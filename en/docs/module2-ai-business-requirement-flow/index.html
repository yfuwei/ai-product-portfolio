01 · Business Problem

Enterprises do not lack feedback. They lack a running mechanism for business signals.

Sales, support, service, quality, CRM, and meetings generate business signals every day, but those signals often stay scattered across systems and channels instead of entering product, quality, sales, supply chain, and management workflows.

Scattered signals

Too many entry points

Feedback is distributed across sales, support, service, meetings, and CRM.

Mixed types

Signals and noise blur together

Requirements, issues, risks, sentiment, and noise often appear in the same stream.

Unclear owner

Responsibility is ambiguous

It is not obvious whether product, quality, sales, supply chain, or operations should own the issue.

Hard to track

Status lives in meetings and chats

Progress depends on group chats, spreadsheets, and manual reminders.

Late review

Management sees summaries too late

Retrospectives happen after the signal has already lost operational value.

No AI: how business signals get lost

Sales / support / CRM

Feedback enters different systems and communication channels.

Meetings / sheets / chat

Signals are temporarily organized with inconsistent wording.

Manual judgment

Priority and ownership depend on experience.

Manual follow-up

Blocks and delays are difficult to expose.

After-the-fact summary

Trends are reviewed too late to become a running mechanism.

02 · Running Scenario

How does one customer signal become organizational action?

A customer concern about rear-seat comfort looks small in one channel. Across sales, CRM, support, regional meetings, and market observations, it may become a product experience issue plus a sales conversion risk.

Signal sources

Sales + CRM + support + meetings

Family users repeatedly mention rear-seat space and comfort concerns.

AI aggregation

Identify the business theme

AI groups similar feedback and recognizes it as a product experience issue plus sales conversion risk.

Card and review

Generate draft, then human confirms

Humans confirm priority, evidence sufficiency, and responsible teams.

Routing

Move into action

Product, sales operations, and marketing support can form follow-up actions and review inputs.

03 · Before / After AI

From scattered feedback to business closure.

AI does not replace final business decisions. It acts as signal identifier, classifier, evidence extractor, card draft generator, routing suggester, and risk reminder. Humans confirm category, priority, ownership, process entry, external conclusion, task closure, and rule adoption.

Before AI

  1. Multi-source business signals
  2. Manual collection
  3. Manual issue classification
  4. Meeting coordination
  5. Manual routing to departments
  6. Chat / spreadsheet / meeting-note tracking
  7. Weekly or monthly review
  8. Partial learning, many signals lost

After AI

  1. Multi-source business signals
  2. AI identification / classification / clustering
  3. Evidence extraction and similar issue matching
  4. Requirement / issue / risk card draft
  5. Human confirmation and correction
  6. Routing to responsible teams
  7. Status tracking and risk reminders
  8. Operational insight and rule learning
  9. Feedback into classification rules and handling paths

04 · AI Intervention Points

AI pre-identifies signals. Humans confirm and decide.

Signal identification

Separate useful feedback from noise

AI detects whether incoming text contains actionable business signals.

Classification and clustering

Group similar issues

AI classifies requirements, issues, risks, and recurring themes.

Evidence extraction

Keep the source visible

AI extracts original snippets and context for review.

Routing suggestion

Suggest but do not dispatch automatically

AI proposes responsible teams and next steps; humans confirm ownership.

05 · Lightweight AI Business Signal Layer

A connecting layer, not a heavy enterprise platform.

This project does not rebuild CRM, support, quality, or project-management systems. It adds a lightweight AI business signal layer between existing systems to support cross-source aggregation, AI structuring, evidence tracing, human routing, status sync, rule iteration, and management review.

Existing systems

CRM / support / quality / meetings

Keep the systems that already manage local objects and records.

AI signal layer

Aggregate and structure

Connect signals across sources, extract evidence, and prepare reviewable objects.

Confirmable objects

Requirement / issue / risk cards

Turn scattered feedback into objects that can be reviewed, routed, and tracked.

Organizational response

Product / sales / quality / management

Move from feedback into responsible action and review.

06 · Core Product Responsibility Unit

From signal pool to organizational action loop.

Input layer

Multi-source business signal pool

Capability: collect sales, support, quality, CRM, meetings, and regional feedback.

Role: keep source and original evidence.

Identification layer

AI identification and classification

Capability: classify, cluster, tag, extract evidence, and identify recurring themes.

Role: separate signal from noise.

Object layer

Requirement / issue / risk cards

Capability: card type, evidence, impact scope, suggested owner.

Role: make feedback confirmable and trackable.

Review layer

Human confirmation

Capability: confirm type, priority, owner, and process entry.

Role: keep judgment and responsibility with people.

Execution layer

Routing and status tracking

Capability: owner, task board, delay reminders, blockers, closure record.

Role: reduce uncertainty around who is handling what.

Review layer

Operational insight and rule learning

Capability: recurring issues, response efficiency, closure rate, trend change.

Role: feed rules, handling paths, and management review.
